Session 19

2025 03 01

Esmerelda and us have just fought a bunch of wolf people at the causeway and left E with a prisoner. She's still a bit antagonistic to us so we head off and leave her to her interrogation.

We travel east toward Vallaki until dusk. Thalion and Banhammer observe signs of a large predatory creature hunting in this region but we decide to camp rather than travel in the dark.

During Charity's watch there's an odd change to the nature of the night. Charity wakes everybody up. They're a little grumpy. No-one else can work out what's up but Charity decides to sneak into the woods and do a wide circle of our camp.

She's just heading out from camp when she catches some reflected light and hears a clicking noise. It's a giant spider in a tree. She immediately heads back to camp but on the way back triggers some web triplines and starts to hear clicking from multiple directions.

She calls warnings to her group and we all get ready for a spider fight. Charity is caught with a web before she gets back to the group but Thalion and Banhammer arrive moments later and the spiders have a fight on their many hands that they quickly lose.

Charity takes a chunk of the spider and hang it on a branch near the camp, and the group turn back in.

Next morning we head for Vallaki, passing through the four way crossroads with the fallen four way sign. As we cross the near-by bridge we look north up the river to the lake in the distance. We see huge bolts of lightning striking the mountain beyond the lake. The lightning hits five or six times in the same place.

We feel like this might be interesting to remember later but for now we join with a few other travellers arriving at Vallaki. Tomorrow is the Festival of the Blazing Sun and we're ushered through the gate without any questioning.

We decide to visit St Andrals, where we left Ireena. it's front door is swinging on a broken hinge. We call for Fr Lucien through the open door. An altarboy glances at us and runs off, returning a moment later with Lucien. We tell him how we ran into Ireena on the road (but not Ismark) and how she had been traded to Strahd by Kresk's burgermeister.

He tells us of the church's attack by Strahd's minions, and that Ireena fled. Ismark stayed to defend the church, and Lucien believes he was killed and the minions took his corpse.

We didn't hear anything about St Andral's bones on our journeys, so there's not much else we can do for Fr Lucien. We ask about his communication with the Abbey, since he said he's lost communication with the Abbey "a while ago". He says that he's been exchanging occasional formal letters of faith with the Abbot but has never had any insight into the day-to-day operation of the Abbey.

We decide to visit Blinsky next and head to his shop. As we walk through the town we notice again the vibe where population aren't on board with the "all will be well" motto that the town's Burghermeister is promoting, and instead we see brittle reluctant compliance with the expected joviality.

Thalion and Charity encourage Aryn to remember that festivals need bagpipes, and Aryn cheers up a little.

Blinsky's shop is closed "on festival business" so we head for the Bluewater Inn. There are some ravens outside, flags and bunting around, and inside it's not very busy. The posh rich kids Aryn crop dusted a bit are here, as is Rictavio.

We head over to talk to Danica and let her know that we'd liberated the Wizards of Wine winery. Danica advises that she's received a shipment via Elvis's brother, and flashes us a were-raven sign to let us know she's in with the Keepers of the Feather.

Rictavio starts in on our wine jug and regales us with the story of the Clockwork Bride of Lamordia.

Ah, Lamordia! A land so cold even the smiles freeze before reaching the eyes. I was invited there to perform for a brilliant doctor — brilliant in the way lightning is brilliant when it strikes a tree.

The good doctor had constructed a bride from brass, bone, and what I sincerely hope was donated material. Alas! She lacked spirit. So who did he summon to inspire animation? Rictavio, of course! I sang to her of distant lands, of passion, of applause! And do you know what happened? She rose from her slab, eyes glowing like twin lanterns, and promptly tried to strangle her creator.

Such enthusiasm! I escaped by vaulting through a laboratory window while she dismantled the furniture. A successful debut performance, I’d say. The doctor disagreed. Bitter fellow.

Charity asks Rictavio when he'll be next setting forth to collect more amazing stories. He's glum for a second because he's stuck here like everyone, but we cheer him up with a heavily fictionalised version of our battle on the mountain of barbarous spirits against a giant animated tree that we fought with an axe we found buried in it's own leg, and when the sun rose our enemies turned to stone.

Aryn punctuates her parts of the story with bagpipes and earns general applause from the group, aside from the noble scions who are cynical about it.

We ask Danica (given she send him lunch the last time we were here) where we might run into Blinsky this evening, but she says he's hard to track down, and she sends him lunch as a kind of welfare check.

Charity asks for a spare carrot from Danica to take to Sir Carrots which Danica manages to find.
